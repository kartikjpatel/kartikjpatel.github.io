About Me

Curious engineer.
Hands-on builder.

I am a mechatronics engineer pursuing my MSc in Mechanical Engineering at Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e), specializing in Control Systems Technology. I learn best by building things and then making them behave reliably in the real world.

My hands-on experience includes real-time system validation at ASML, where I bridged physical hardware and Simulink models through systematic calibration of spring force, motor constants, time delays, and feedforward control loops. This kind of hardware-software interface work is where I feel most engaged.

Beyond validation, I have developed smart material actuators (bachelor's thesis, 10/10), worked on thermal simulation and CFD, and I am currently building frequency-domain algorithms for fractional-order system identification as part of my master's thesis.

Outside engineering, I serve as an International Student Ambassador at TU/e, representing the university at international events and mentoring incoming students.
